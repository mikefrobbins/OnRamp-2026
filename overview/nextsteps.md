# Next steps

![ms powershell](../images/powershell-hero.jpg)

Thank you for joining us this year. We hope OnRamp was a valuable experience. It certainly was for
us. Learning never stops, and everyone has something to contribute.

As you move forward, here are a few next steps:

- **Apply what you've learned:** Put your new skills to work in your day-to-day role or personal
  projects.

- **Share your knowledge:** Help others grow. Even showing a coworker a single command can make an
  impact. Help others grow. Even showing a coworker a single command can make an impact.

- **Keep learning:** OnRamp introduced several new topics. Pick one that excites you or supports
  your work and go deeper.

- **Engage with the PowerShell community:** Ask questions in Slack or start conversations in the
  [discussion section][discussions] of the OnRamp GitHub repository.

## Online resources

Start by bookmarking [Microsoft's official PowerShell documentation][powershell-docs].

Additional community resources:

- [PowerShell.org][powershell-org]
- [PowerShell.org Forums][powershell-org-forums]
- [PowerShell community starter pack on Bluesky][starter-pack]
- [PowerShell Discord channel][discord]
- [PowerShell Slack channel][slack]
- [PlanetPowerShell][planet-powershell]
- [PowerShell Podcast][powershell-podcast]

The Slack and Discord channels share the same backend. Use whichever platform you prefer.

Follow the `#PowerShell` tag on social media to stay connected with the community.

## Books

To continue building your skills:

- [Learn PowerShell in a Month of Lunches][powershell-month-lunches]
- [Learn PowerShell Scripting in a Month of Lunches][scripting-month-lunches]
- [The PowerShell Scripting and Toolmaking Book][scripting-toolmaking]

If your focus is Windows PowerShell, consider [PowerShell 101: The No-Nonsense Guide to Windows
PowerShell][powershell101].

## Videos

- Subscribe to the [PowerShell.org YouTube channel][youtube] for recordings of Summit sessions.

Ask peers about their favorite learning resources. Learning from multiple voices strengthens
understanding, but remember, the best way to learn is by building.

Thank you for trusting us with your time this week. We hope OnRamp was a valuable investment in your
journey.
